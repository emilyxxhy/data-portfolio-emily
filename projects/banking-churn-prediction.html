Banking Churn Prediction

Leveraging Machine Learning and Interpretability to Reduce Customer Attrition.

#Python #XGBoost #SHAP #Classification

1. Project Overview & Problem

A mid-sized regional bank faced unsustainable customer churn, leading to significant Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and lost Lifetime Value (LTV). The goal was to build a predictive model to identify high-risk customers **before** they churn, allowing the Relationship Management team to intervene proactively with targeted offers.

2. Data & Methodology

  • **Data Source:** Synthetic dataset of 10,000 bank customers with 14 features (e.g., Credit Score, Age, Balance, Tenure).
  • **Data Prep:** Handled categorical encoding, applied standardization, and addressed class imbalance using **SMOTE** to ensure the model wouldn't ignore the minority (churning) class.
  • **Modeling:** Chose **XGBoost** due to its superior performance on structured data and its ability to handle complex, non-linear relationships, outperforming baseline Logistic Regression by 10%.
  • **Evaluation Metric:** Prioritized **Precision** (minimizing false positives) over Recall. The cost of incorrectly offering an intervention to a loyal customer (False Positive) was deemed lower than missing a churning customer (False Negative) for this project.

**Actionable Insight:** By using **SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)** values, we identified that 'Number of Products' and 'Is Active Member' were the strongest indicators of loyalty, while 'Balance' was a high-risk churn driver. This insight allowed the bank to design a targeted offer for customers with low product counts and high balances.
